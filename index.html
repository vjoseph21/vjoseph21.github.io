About

Data science at the intersection of research and real-world impact.

I'm a Data Science Research Manager at the Institute for Climate and Sustainable Growth at the University of Chicago, where I lead technical development on environmental initiatives backed by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), NASA, and multilateral organizations such as the World Bank — building production-grade systems that support large-scale causal research and policy evaluations across the globe.

My work sits at the intersection of AI systems, environmental data, and public policy. I lead teams designing and deploying agentic pipelines, geospatial ML models, and randomized controlled trial infrastructure that generate evidence capable of informing real regulatory decisions.

I hold a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering from the National Institute of Technology Karnataka and a Master's in Computational Analysis and Public Policy from the University of Chicago — training that gave me both an engineer's instinct for building robust systems and a policy researcher's commitment to evidence that matters.
